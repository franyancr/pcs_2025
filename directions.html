Directions

The PCS 2025 Workshop will take place at two different locations on the University of New Mexico main campus in Albuquerque. On Thursday (09/18), the workshop will take place in room 1044 (ground floor) of the Centennial Engineering Center building, located at 300 Redondo W Dr. (see map below). On Friday and Saturday (09/19-09/20), the workshop will take place in room 2540 (second floor) of the Physics & Astronomy and Interdisciplinary Science (PAIS) building, located at 210 Yale Blvd NE (see map below).

To reach UNM's main campus from the Parq Central hotel, participants can either walk (20 mins) or take the free rapid transit bus route 766/777 (direction Uptown Transit Center or Central & Tramway) from the center platform located at the intersection of Central Avenue and Walter street (located 3 blocks west of the hotel) and getting off either at the UNM/CNM station (Thursday) or the UNM-Popejoy station (Friday/Saturday). The hotel also offers a complimentary shuttle that can take PCS participants to UNM's main campus. Availabiltiy of the shuttle is however limited; inquire at the hotel's front desk for details. The PCS local organizing committee will also pick up some out-of-state participants from the hotel each morning of the workshop.

Location for Thursday September 18

To easily access room 1044 (ground floor), enter by the double doors on the Southeast corner of the Centennial Engineering Center (CEC) building. The workshop room will then be the first room on your right. Note that most entrance doors to the CEC building are always locked, except for the double doors on the Southeast corner, which are open during regular business hours.

Location for Friday and Saturday September 19-20

To access the Physics & Astronomy and Interdisciplinary Science (PAIS) Bldg, enter via the inner courtyard doors, which can be accessed via the west side of the building. The workshop room (2540) is located on the second floor. To access it, cross the PAIS lobby and find a set of glass doors marked "Card Access Only". Someone will be there to open the doors for you. Then, climb the stairs to the left of the glass doors to the second floor. At the top of the stairs, take a left and walk all the way down the long corridor. Room 2540 will be to your left. Note that most entrance doors to the PAIS building are always locked, except for the doors located in the inner courtyard, which are open during regular business hours.
